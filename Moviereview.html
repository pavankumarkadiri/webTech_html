Review:

Kushi is a romantic drama that beautifully captures the essence of love and friendship. Pawan Kalyan's charismatic performance as Siddhu is a standout, bringing charm and energy to the screen. Bhumika Chawla, as the female lead Madhu, complements him perfectly with her subtle and graceful portrayal.

The film's story revolves around Siddhu and Madhu, two friends who are unaware of their love for each other. Their journey is filled with misunderstandings, ego clashes, and eventually, the realization of their deep feelings for each other. The narrative is engaging, with well-placed humor and emotional moments that strike a chord with the audience.

The music by Mani Sharma is another highlight, with songs like "Ammaye Sannaga" and "Ye Mera Jaha" still being popular among fans. The chemistry between the lead pair, combined with the captivating screenplay and direction by S. J. Suryah, makes Kushi a memorable film that resonates with audiences even today.

Overall, Kushi is a delightful film that showcases Pawan Kalyan's versatility and remains a favorite in his filmography. It's a must-watch for fans of romantic dramas.
